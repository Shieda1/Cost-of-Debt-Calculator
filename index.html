What is the cost of debt?
The cost of debt is the interest rate that a company pays on it's borrowed cash such as loans. There are two types of cost of debt the first is before tax cost of debt and the second is after tax cost of debt.
How to calculate cost of debt?
To calculate cost of debt you should use the following formula:
cost of debt = total interest / total debt
Why use cost of debt?
The cost of debt is used to measure the overall interest rate that is being paid by a company to borrow cash and investors use it to meausre the risk level of a company. High cost of debt indicates that the company is risky for investing.
